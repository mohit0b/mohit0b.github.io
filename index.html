Demystifying Supplier Tiers

Think of Tier-2 as your supplier’s supplier. Seeing beyond Tier-1 keeps surprises out of your production calendar.

Anchor (Original Equipment Manufacturer)

You, the brand on the product

Automakers such as Aura Motors assemble the finished vehicle and own the customer relationship.

Tier-1 Supplier

Your direct vendor

Provides ready-to-install modules like battery packs or infotainment systems. Example: Foxconn assembling iPhones for Apple.

Tier-2 Supplier

The danger zone

Feeds Tier-1 with critical sub-components (e.g., Samsung supplying OLED screens). Disruptions here ripple to you fast.

Tier-3 Supplier

Raw inputs

Source materials such as Corning’s Gorilla Glass or lithium miners. Often invisible unless actively mapped.

Blind Spot Example

A Tier-2 factory fire in China halts lithium cell shipments. Your Tier-1 supplier in Pune runs dry in days, impacting your EV launch.

LinkNode Utility

LinkNode unmasks Tier-2/Tier-3 relationships, continuously scoring risk so you can reroute orders before a slowdown hits your line.
